Evelynn Preciado

Video Producer

in LinkedIn ✉ Email ☎ Call

Evelynn is a bilingual documentary producer with over a decade of experience illuminating America's complex narratives—from indigenous perspectives in the Black Hills and El Malpais to pivotal moments at Antietam, Yorktown, and the Harriet Tubman Underground Railroad. Her recent work with the American Battle Monuments Commission spanned 11 countries across four continents, capturing the quiet dignity of memorial sites worldwide. Working in English and Spanish, she creates educational content that honors marginalized voices and challenges audiences to see familiar histories through new eyes.

Virginia 2019 23 min Post-Production Producer

A film about the site of the Yorktown Battlefield, Colonial America, and the Revolutionary War's last major battle, led by General George Washington and allied forces, resulting in the surrender of General Cornwallis' British Army on October 19, 1781

Harriet Tubman: Soldier of Freedom

Maryland 2021 17 min Producer & Production Manager

In this heart wrenching yet triumphant film, Harriet Tubman: Soldier of Freedom tells the compelling story of Tubman's life as an enslaved person, as well as her pursuit of freedom for herself and her loved ones. The story is told on location on the Eastern Shore of Maryland.
